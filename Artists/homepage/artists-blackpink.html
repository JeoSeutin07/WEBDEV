About BLACKPINK


Blackpink is a South Korean girl group formed by YG Entertainment. The group consists of four members: Jisoo, Jennie, Rosé, and Lisa. They made their debut in 2016 with their single album "Square One" and have since become one of the most popular K-pop groups in the world. Known for their unique style and catchy music, Blackpink has broken numerous records and received several awards since their debut. Their music videos have garnered billions of views on YouTube, making them one of the most-watched music acts on the platform. In addition to their music, Blackpink is also known for their fashion and beauty, with each member having their own unique style.

Since then, Blackpink has released numerous chart-topping hits such as "Kill This Love," "DDU-DU DDU-DU," "How You Like That," and "Lovesick Girls." They have collaborated with international artists such as Lady Gaga and Selena Gomez, and have been featured in popular television shows such as "The Late Late Show with James Corden" and "Good Morning America." Blackpink has also become known for their fashion sense, often donning designer outfits and appearing on the covers of fashion magazines. Their popularity has made them one of the most influential girl groups in the world, with a massive following on social media platforms such as Instagram and YouTube.
